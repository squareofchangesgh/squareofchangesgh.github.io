Polina

Saw what happened at the Square of Changes on November 15

On that day, my dad was seized, my best friend got arrested and beaten, and later I found six or seven familiar names on the lists of detainees.

Being around the Square of Changes was scary. Not only was I afraid of leaving the apartment but also looking out of the window.

In the basements of the houses nearby about a hundred people were hiding from the security forces – we ​​were worrying about their lives a lot but were unable to help and felt powerless.

I also remember my heart breaking into pieces when I saw the lanterns destroyed and the flowers brought in memory of Roma stomped with the riot police’ boots.

The Encirclement of “PP”

During the dispersal, several hundred people managed to hide in the entrances, many were allowed to hide in the apartments by the Square of Changes residents. Security forces broke into flats and checked the people’s registration documents. They were looking for protesters and journalists. No one could either enter or leave the encircled area without showing their documents.

On that day, two journalists of Belsat TV channel, Yekaterina Andreyeva and Darya Chultsova, were detained in one of the houses surrounding the “PP”. The girls are now serving a sentence in a colony – two years for “inciting to riots”. Ekaterina was to be released in September 2022, but in July she got convicted again. The court sentenced her for 8 more years for treason. The trial was held behind closed doors, so the details of the case are unknown. Andreeva's lawyer is under a non-disclosure agreement.
