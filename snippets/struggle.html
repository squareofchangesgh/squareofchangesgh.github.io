The Square of Changes residents wash the mural with the DJs after it was painted over by the housing and communal services employees

01/09/2020

Yan

Was working on the mural

Initially, we were printing the DJs images on paper and then attaching them to the wall with white glue.

We knew that the mural could be quickly painted over, so we took a few copies from the printing studio at once. It took about 10-15 minutes to attach a new image to the wall.

We had our own neighborhood team monitoring the situation nonstop, thus we knew about the location of all the cops and tikhars in the area of several blocks.

We still have printed copies. They are waiting for their moment to resurrect on the Square of Changes.

●

Fight for the Mural

The mural at the Square of Changes was destroyed literally every day. People from public utility services, in balaclavas and usually guarded by the police, painted over the “DJs” and cut off the ribbons. Day and night, the mural was attacked by the unknown who destroyed the graffiti without explanation.

However, the locals quickly washed the wall and restored the mural. At first, the restoration process was done secretly: people were afraid of being arrested for trying to keep the image on the wall. But on September 1, 2020, the residents of the neighboring houses went down to the backyard in broad daylight to once again restore the destroyed. In total, the mural was recreated more than 20 times.
