“It is only this summer that we met” is one of the slogans of the protests that swept across Belarus after the presidential elections in August 2020.

Recognizing one other in the streets, the sense of belonging to the community larger than one’s family or a professional circle were intense sensations that felt as new to huge masses of people. City dwellers were often disconnected and answering the question, “Do you know your neighbors’ names?” they would have probably said “no”. Until recently.

The backyard communities movement became a significant phenomenon in 2020 in Belarus. Hundreds of thousands of people who took to streets as one whole joining city marches and rallies returned to their "sleeping areas" and continued their activities there – getting to know their neighbors.

The Square of Changes, or, as the residents themselves call it, “PP” [as the abbreviation of “Ploshcha Peramen” – “the Square of Changes” in Belarussian. Translator’s note], is just one example of such activism. However, the story of the residents’ resistance and their courageous struggle for their mural, their friends and their community has revealed a larger clash – a conflict of values, attitudes, and social groups.

●

Officials and state-run media are increasingly trying to distort the recent past, including the history of the "rebellious Minsk backyard." We have created this project to preserve the timeline and the names of brave heroes and heroines of the Square of Changes. Our team is committed to preventing history from being rewritten by those who illegally seized power in the summer of 2020.
