How Did the "DJs of Changes" Appear?

The Start of “PP”

On the photo:

People at the backyard of the Square of Changes shine their torches and sing songs

02/09/2020

Through documentary photography, the project shows the rise of the backyard community. It presents a story about a playground that turned into a place of confrontation, self-organization, manifestation of solidarity and political activity, and then – into a memorial to the deceased Roman Bondarenko and finally – into a symbol of the Belarusian protests of 2020-2021.


‍

●

During the election campaign, state services obstructed Lukashenka's rivals. For example, on August 6, 2020, Svetlana Tikhanovskaya was planning to meet voters in Kievsky Garden Square. A few days before the announced rally, the city authorities had decided to hold their event on the same site. They brought the equipment and started preparing for the performance. Tikhanovskaya's headquarters announced that Svetlana would still come at the appointed time and participate in the celebration. Several thousand Belarusians gathered to support her.

Around 7 pm, “Our hearts demand changes!” suddenly started via the loudspeakers – people began to applaud, shout out slogans, and words of approval. Since Perestroika, the song of the rock musician Viktor Tsoi has been known as a hymn to freedom and opposition to power. It appeared that Tsoi’s hit had been played by the sound engineers Kirill Galanov and Vlad Sokolovsky. The guys were forced to work on a public holiday, but they decided to support the protest. A photograph depicting the DJs with their raised hands with white ribbons quickly spread around the Belarusian and foreign media.

Immediately after the event, Kirill and Vlad were arrested and got a 10-days’ sentence. The guys missed the presidential election that ended in massive fraud and the largest protests in the history of Belarus.

The "DJs of Changes" became a new symbol of protest. Their act inspired many Belarusians. A stylized image depicting the young men appeared on the playground of the yard at the intersection of Smorgovskiy Trakt and Chervyakova Street.

On August 16, the most massive rally in the history of modern Belarus was held in Minsk near the Stela. 300,000-500,000 people are estimated to have taken part in it. Residents of the blocks of flats surrounding the backyard were also there, and after the demonstration they launched a Telegram chat as a convenient platform for negotiating various activities held in the yard, communicating, and discussing the events that unfolded in the country. It turned out that neighbors who had known nothing about one another could be friends. People were overcoming atomization, making sure that the supporters of changes were in the majority.

A few days later, the playground was named “The Square of Changes” and grew into a center of attraction for citizens who wanted a change of power.
