On August 16, 2021 the court gave its verdict to Stepan Latypov. He was accused of creating a backyard Telegram-chat, holding a workshop where protest symbols were made, and resisting arrest on September 15. Moreover, the man was claimed guilty of misleading his customers when providing chemical plant protection services.

Stepan Latypov pleaded not guilty and refused to testify. He was sentenced to 8 years and 6 months of a reinforced regime colony.



●

A Suicide Attempt

Stepan was accused of taking part in mass riots. However, just four days later, the situation changed: state TV channels reported that he was suspected of organizing an illegal channel for dangerous chemicals shipment to the country. Pro-government journalists claimed that the man had intended to use poisonous substances against policemen.

On June 1, 2021 the trial started. Stepan Latypov was brought to the court room with obvious signs of beatings. During the hearing, the man said that brutal pressure had been exerted to make him give false confessions. Stepan stepped on the bench and said, "GUBOP [the Main Directorate for Combating Organized Crime – Translator’s note] threatened to press criminal charges against my relatives and neighbors if I did not admit guilt," and pierced his neck with a pen. The unconscious man was hospitalized, however during the night after the operation the security forces took him to Detention Center 1 on Volodarsky Street.
