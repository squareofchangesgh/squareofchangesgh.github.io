A Day at the Square of Changes


Tikhar


Tikhar is a law enforcement agency employee that carries no identification marks. As a rule, tikhars work at rallies and protests in plain clothes that do not draw extra attention (dark jackets and caps) and cover their faces with balaclavas or masks to remain anonymous. They can collect intelligence information shooting videos with small handheld cameras or mobile phones.

At rallies, their tasks include surveillance and detention of protesters. Their work is coordinated by the police, GUBOPiK, KGB or other power structures. In courts, tikhars often give testimonies as witnesses on the prosecution side, still keeping their identities secret. Moreover, they are allowed to wear balaclavas or masks during court hearings.


Security Forces Vehicles


Security forces transport is armored green MAZs, blue-and-white paddy wagons, mini-buses (often silver), ordinary civilian buses and cars. They are meant for personnel transportation, but are often used in the detention of protesters.

Green vans and minibuses became one of the symbols of the Lukashenka regime terror. During the protests, in these vehicles detainees were severely beaten.

On September 9, around the Square of Changes, one could see almost all the types of military vehicles. Two minibuses with unidentified security forces members, a green MAZ with riot police, a yellow bus with policemen and a police car arrived to disperse the concert.
